First Things First

Congratulations, you're getting a puppy! Many of our friends have asked us for advice on raising a dog, so we've decided to consolidate the highlights here. We've learned a lot from Watson, and are delighted to share. That's him on the day we first got him!

Before we ever brought Watson home, however, we did our homework. Two books we found particularly useful are How To Raise The Perfect Dog and Cesar's Rules by Cesar Millan. Both are a very easy read. They do a good job not only providing techniques for taking good care of your pup, but also explaining the principles behind training techniques. You'll feel much more prepared to be a great dog parent after reading these books!

If you're anything like we are, you'll soon find yourself buying way too much stuff for your new "child." Trust us, this is normal. A couple of essentials are particularly important to get beforehand. For crate training, find a comfortable, size appropraite travel crate, like this one. You'll read all about the importance of crate training in Cesar's books, but suffice to say that it's incredibly helpful for potty training, and being able to travel with your pup later. While you're at it, pick up a pen like this to set up in your home. Puppies get overwhelmed by open spaces, so a smaller space will help the little one feel safe.

Getting Through the Teenage Years

Once you and your little bud have gotten through early puppyhood together, it's time for doggy teenage years. Just like human teenage years, these are formative, exciting, and challenging. From 6 months until about 3 years old, the name of the game will be patience and consistency.

One of the most important things for your pup to work on early is socialization. You should begin exposing them to as many different people as possible (kids, adults, men, women, etc), as soon as possible. Keep this up from puppyhood to the teenage years to help raise a dog that is friendly to all humans it meets! Just be careful to limit squealing and excitement. Although this can be a tough one, especially with an adorable puppy, people tend to understand when you ask them to act calmly so your pup can learn to be calm too.

A great way to learn socialization and obedience is by enrolling in puppy school. Sirius Dog Training worked well for us - check them out if you're in the Bay Area. Developing a training routine will engage your dog and help him/her develop a strong bond with you. Of course, the most important part will be to keep up the training routine you learn in puppy school in the months and years afterward. This is all part of the consistency and structure that will help your dog feel balanced and be well behaved.

Your Perfect Dog

Time flies! Before you know it, the confused little fluff ball you once brought home will turn into a proud, loyal companion. People often tell us that Watson is the perfect dog. Of course, the truth is, he is just the perfect dog for us. If you put in the time and discipline into the early months and years, you'll also raise the best dog you could ever imagine.

So, what now? Is your job done? Well, sort of. The hardest part is over. But now, it's time to make sure both you and your dog get the most out of your relationship. Make time each day to bond with your dog by taking him/her on walks (at least one 45-60min walk per day). Get to know the best doggie play spots in your area, like parks, hiking trails, or beaches. Fort Funston and Crissy Field are two of our favorites. Keep challenging your bud through continued training. This can be as simple as letting him/her earn yummy treats by going through the basics (sit, stay, leave-it), or as adventurous as going to an agility course. Or share the joy your dog brings you with others by volunteering together through a therapy dog program. Whatever you end up choosing, always remember to be just as much of a best friend to your dog as he or she is to you.

That's all we have for now! It's such a joy to bring a dog into your life. Watson has become an integral part of our family, and we can't wait to see how your dog becomes a part of yours.
